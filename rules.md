## Articles (Articoli)

### Definite Articles (Articoli Determinativi)

Italian has specific definite articles based on gender, number, and the beginning sound of the following word.

### Usage Rules (Regole d'Uso)

#### Masculine Singular (Maschile Singolare)

- **il**: Use before most consonants
  - *il greco, il tedesco, il russo*
- **lo**: Use before:
  - s + consonant: *lo spagnolo, lo svedese*
  - z: *lo zaino* (backpack)
  - gn: *lo gnomo* (gnome)
  - ps: *lo psicologo* (psychologist)
  - x, y: *lo xilofono* (xylophone)
- **l'**: Use before vowels
  - *l'italiano, l'inglese, l'europeo, l'americano*

#### Masculine Plural (Maschile Plurale)

- **i**: Use before most consonants
  - *i greci, i tedeschi, i russi*
- **gli**: Use before:
  - Vowels: *gli italiani, gli inglesi, gli europei*
  - s + consonant: *gli spagnoli, gli svedesi*
  - z, gn, ps, x, y: *gli zaini, gli gnomi*

#### Feminine Singular (Femminile Singolare)

- **la**: Use before consonants
  - *la greca, la tedesca, la spagnola, la russa*
- **l'**: Use before vowels
  - *l'italiana, l'inglese, l'europea, l'americana*

#### Feminine Plural (Femminile Plurale)

- **le**: Use before ALL feminine plural words
  - *le greche, le italiane, le tedesche, le spagnole*

### Adjective Agreement (Accordo degli Aggettivi)

**Î¤Î¿ ÎµÏ€Î¯Î¸ÎµÏ„Î¿ Î±ÎºÎ¿Î»Î¿Ï…Î¸ÎµÎ¯ Ï„Î¿ Î³Î­Î½Î¿Ï‚ ÎºÎ±Î¹ Ï„Î¿Î½ Î±ÏÎ¹Î¸Î¼ÏŒ Ï„Î¿Ï… Î¿Ï…ÏƒÎ±ÏƒÏ„Î¹ÎºÎ¿Ï!** (The adjective follows the gender and number of the noun!)

In Italian, adjectives must agree with the noun in both gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural).

- *un ragazzo italiano* (a masculine Italian boy)
- *una ragazza italiana* (a feminine Italian girl)
- *i ragazzi italiani* (masculine Italian boys)
- *le ragazze italiane* (feminine Italian girls)

### Nouns Ending in -ma (ÎŸÏ…ÏƒÎ¹Î±ÏƒÏ„Î¹ÎºÎ¬ ÏƒÎµ -ma)

**Î¤Î± Î¿Ï…ÏƒÎ¹Î±ÏƒÏ„Î¹ÎºÎ¬ ÏƒÎµ -ma ÎµÎ¯Î½Î±Î¹ Î±ÏÏƒÎµÎ½Î¹ÎºÎ¬!** (Nouns ending in -ma are masculine!)

Nouns ending in **-ma** are masculine, even though they end in "a" (which typically indicates feminine).

Examples:

- *il problema* (the problem) - masculine, not feminine
- *il tema* (the theme/topic)
- *il sistema* (the system)
- *il programma* (the program)
- *il dramma* (the drama)

Plural forms end in **-mi**:

- *i problemi* (the problems)
- *i temi* (the themes)
- *i sistemi* (the systems)

### Nouns Ending in -e (ÎŸÏ…ÏƒÎ¹Î±ÏƒÏ„Î¹ÎºÎ¬ ÏƒÎµ -e)

Nouns ending in **-e** can be either masculine or feminine. You must learn the gender of each noun individually.

**Masculine examples:**

- *il padre* (the father)
- *il nome* (the name)
- *il fiore* (the flower)
- *il cane* (the dog)
- *il francese* (the French man)

**Feminine examples:**

- *la madre* (the mother)
- *la chiave* (the key)
- *la notte* (the night)
- *la classe* (the class)
- *la francese* (the French woman)

Both masculine and feminine nouns ending in **-e** form their plural with **-i**:

- *il padre â†’ i padri* (masculine)
- *la madre â†’ le madri* (feminine)
- *il francese â†’ i francesi* (masculine)
- *la francese â†’ le francesi* (feminine)
