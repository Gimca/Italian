## Patterns to Remember (Schemi da Ricordare)

### Basic Numbers (1-20)

- **0-10**: Learn by heart (zero, uno, due, tre, quattro, cinque, sei, sette, otto, nove, dieci)
- **11-16**: Irregular forms ending in -dici (undici, dodici, tredici, quattordici, quindici, sedici)
- **17-19**: dieci + asse/o + number (diciassette, diciotto, diciannove)

### Compound Numbers (21-99)

- **Pattern**: Decade + unit number (written as one word)
- **Drop final vowel before vowels**:
  - venti + otto = vent**otto** (not ventiotto)
  - trenta + uno = trent**uno** (not trentauno)
  - cinquanta + otto = cinquant**otto**
- **Accent on numbers ending in 3**: ventitrÃ©, trentatrÃ©, quarantatrÃ©, etc.
- **All decades 20-90 end in -anta** (except venti which ends in -i)

### Hundreds (100-900)

- **100 = cento** (NO "un" before it)
- **200-900**: number + cento (one word): duecento, trecento, quattrocento...
- **Compounds**: Just add the rest: centoventicinque (125), duecentoquaranta (240)

### Thousands (1,000+)

- **1,000 = mille** (singular, irregular - NO "un" before it)
- **2,000+ = -mila** (plural): duemila, tremila, quattromila...
- **Million = un milione** (requires "un")
- **Compounds**: All written as one word: milleduecentotrentaquattro (1,234)

### General Rules

1. **Written as one word**: Italian numbers are written as single words (no hyphens or spaces)
2. **Vowel elision**: Final vowel drops before another vowel (especially before otto and uno)
3. **No "and"**: Unlike English ("one hundred and five"), Italian doesn't use "e" (and) in numbers
4. **Agreement**: Only "uno/una" agrees with gender; all other numbers are invariable
