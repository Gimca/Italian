### Gender Patterns

- Most nationalities ending in **-o** (masculine) change to **-a** (feminine)
  - Example: greco â†’ greca, italiano â†’ italiana
- Nationalities ending in **-e** often stay the same for both genders
  - Example: francese, inglese, albanese, cinese, svedese
- Some have irregular plurals:
  - greco â†’ greci, tedesco â†’ tedeschi (add -h- before -i)

### Important Notes

- These words can function as both **adjectives** and **nouns**
- As adjectives, they describe nationality: *un ragazzo italiano* (an Italian boy)
- As nouns, they refer to the person: *l'italiano parla bene* (the Italian speaks well)
- Language names use the masculine singular form: *parlo italiano* (I speak Italian)
