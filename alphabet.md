## Notes (Note)

**Important points:**

1. **H is always silent** - It's used only to change the sound of C and G (ch, gh) or in the verb "avere" (ho, hai, ha, hanno)

2. **Vowels are always pronounced clearly** - Unlike English, Italian vowels are never silent: a, e, i, o, u

3. **The alphabet has 21 letters** - Not 26 like English or 24 like Greek

4. **Double consonants are pronounced longer** - *casa* (house) vs *cassa* (cash register) - the double 's' is held longer
